The allocation timeline follows a global pattern of mmWave becoming the capacity relief valve for mid-band congestion in high-density deployments, validating the dual-band 5G architecture most operators are now designing toward. Canada's approach also reflects lessons from US mmWave deployment, where early licensing without clear ecosystem readiness led to underutilized spectrum.

The mmWave timeline gives Canadian operators a procurement window to negotiate competitive pricing on mmWave small cell infrastructure before demand peaks — the 12–18 month lead time between spectrum licensing and large-scale deployment is when vendor leverage is lowest and customization options are highest. Enterprise architects designing private 5G networks for Canadian industrial, healthcare, or venue applications should incorporate mmWave capacity planning into their 2025–2026 designs, as the economics of private mmWave 5G become compelling at the throughput densities these bands enable. The more significant implication is that mmWave private 5G in Canada will create competitive pressure on Wi-Fi 6E deployments for indoor enterprise — a technology substitution decision that facilities and network teams need to evaluate jointly.
